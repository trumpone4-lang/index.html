I Owe More Than My Car Is Worth? Can I Refinance?

Free Tools to Run the Numbers

Yes. You absolutely can refinance a car loan if you're upside down. Thousands of people do it every year, and you can too. Being upside down — also called negative equity — means you owe more on your car loan than the vehicle is currently worth. This happens to nearly 27% of people trading in vehicles, with an average negative equity of nearly $7,000.

Key Point #1: Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is what matters most. Most credit unions accept LTV ratios up to 120-130%, meaning you can refinance if you owe up to 30% more than your car's current value.

Key Point #2: Your credit score dramatically affects your refinance options. At 650 credit score (fair credit), you can typically refinance with rates between 8-12% APR. At 600 credit score, expect rates between 12-18% APR.

Key Point #3: Special lenders accept higher LTV ratios. While most lenders cap LTV at 120-125%, some credit unions and online lenders specialize in negative equity refinancing and accept up to 130-150% LTV.

Key Point #4: Making a lump sum payment can help you qualify. Even paying down $1,000 or $2,000 can bring your LTV into an acceptable range.

Key Point #5: A cosigner can make a huge difference. Adding a cosigner with good credit (700+) can lower your interest rate by 3-5% and improve your approval odds significantly.

The calculator below will show you exactly how much you could save by refinancing your upside down car loan. Enter your current loan balance, remaining months, interest rate, and a potential new rate. You will see your current payment, new payment, monthly savings, total interest saved after fees, and your breakeven point.

Key Point #6: Compare total interest saved, not just monthly payment. Some refinance offers lower your monthly payment but extend your term so much that you pay more total interest. The calculator shows you total interest saved after fees so you can make an informed decision.

How I Saved $3,200 Refinancing My Upside Down Car Loan (And You Can Too)

Two years ago, I was staring at a car loan with a 13% interest rate and 48 months remaining. The problem? I owed $18,000 on a car worth only $15,000. I was upside down by $3,000, and my monthly payment was crushing my budget.

My Real Numbers: $18,000 balance | 13% rate → 9% rate | 48 months | $250 refinance fees
Result: Monthly payment dropped from $474 to $448 | Total interest saved after fees: $998 | Breakeven: 10 months

One of the most common questions I heard was about credit scores. People kept asking can i refinance my car with a 600 credit score and can i refinance my car with a 650 credit score. The answer to both is yes, but your rate will vary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you refinance a car loan if your upside down? +
Yes, you can refinance a car loan if you're upside down. Most credit unions accept LTV ratios up to 120-130%.
What are the reasons to refinance your car? +
Lower monthly payment, reduce interest rate, shorten loan term, remove a cosigner.
When's the best time to refinance a car? +
When rates have dropped 1-2% below your current rate and you have at least 24 months remaining.
Can I refinance my car with a 600 credit score? +
Yes, with rates between 12-18% APR. Credit unions are your best option.
Can I refinance my car with a 650 credit score? +
Yes, with rates between 8-12% APR through many lenders.
Can you refinance a buy here pay here car? +
Yes, refinancing a BHPH car can save you thousands due to high interest rates.
Can I refinance a leased car? +
No directly, but you can buy out the lease then refinance.
Can you refinance a car loan with a cosigner? +
Yes, a cosigner can lower your rate by 3-5%.
