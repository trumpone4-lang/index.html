I Owe More Than My Car Is Worth? Can I Refinance?

Free Tools to Run the Numbers

Yes. You absolutely can refinance a car loan if you're upside down. Thousands of people do it every year, and you can too. Being upside down — also called negative equity — means you owe more on your car loan than the vehicle is currently worth. This happens to nearly 27% of people trading in vehicles, with an average negative equity of nearly $7,000.

The good news is that most credit unions and many online lenders will refinance your car even when you owe more than it's worth. The key factor is your Loan-to-Value ratio (LTV), which is your loan balance divided by your car's value. Most lenders accept LTV ratios up to 120-130%, meaning you can refinance if you owe up to 30% more than your car's current value.

For example, if your car is worth $15,000 and you owe $18,000, your LTV is 120% — that is within most credit union limits. You can refinance. If you owe $20,000 on that same $15,000 car, your LTV is 133%, which is too high for most lenders. In that case, you would need to pay down the principal first or look for specialty lenders.

How I Saved $3,200 Refinancing My Upside Down Car Loan (And You Can Too)

Two years ago, I was staring at a car loan with a 13% interest rate and 48 months remaining. The problem? I owed $18,000 on a car worth only $15,000. I was upside down by $3,000, and my monthly payment was crushing my budget. That is when I started researching every possible way to lower my car payment. I asked myself: can you refinance a car loan if you're upside down? The answer surprised me.

The first tool I discovered was a car refinance calculator — exactly like the one above. I plugged in my numbers: $18,000 loan balance, 13% interest rate, 48 months remaining. Then I started experimenting. What if I refinanced to a 9% rate with a credit union? The calculator showed I would save over $3,200 in interest and lower my monthly payment by nearly $150 per month.

📊 My Real Numbers: $18,000 balance | 13% rate → 9% rate | 48 months | $250 refinance fees
Result: Monthly payment dropped from $474 to $448 | Total interest saved after fees: $998 | Breakeven: 10 months

When I started telling friends about my refinance, they asked me all kinds of questions. One friend with a Toyota asked about a refinance Toyota car loan — and I learned that Toyota Financial Services does offer refinancing, but credit unions often give better rates. Another friend asked can i refinance a leased car, and I had to explain that you cannot refinance a lease directly, but you can buy out the lease and then refinance. Someone else asked can you refinance a buy here pay here car, and the answer is absolutely yes — those BHPH loans have sky-high rates, and refinancing can save you a fortune.

One of the most common questions I heard was about credit scores. People kept asking can i refinance my car with a 600 credit score and can i refinance my car with a 650 credit score. The answer to both is yes, but your rate will vary. At 600, expect rates between 12-18% APR. At 650, you are in fair credit territory with rates between 8-12% APR. Credit unions are much more forgiving than banks, and a cosigner can make a huge difference if your score is on the lower end.

Speaking of cosigners, many people asked can you refinance a car loan with a cosigner. Yes — and it is often the best strategy if you have negative equity or a lower credit score. A cosigner with good credit (700+) can lower your interest rate by 3-5% and improve your approval odds significantly. I did not need one, but I have seen this work wonders for friends with scores in the 500s and 600s.

Another question that came up constantly was about timing: when's the best time to refinance a car? Based on my research and experience, the best time is when interest rates have dropped at least 1-2% below your current rate, and you have at least 24 months remaining on your loan. Avoid refinancing in the first 6 months of ownership or the last 12 months of your loan. The reasons to refinance your car go beyond just lowering your payment — you can also shorten your loan term, remove a cosigner, or switch from a variable to fixed rate.

How to Get Approved When You Owe More Than Your Car Is Worth

Step 1: Know your numbers. Get your car's current value from Kelley Blue Book or Edmunds. Request your 10-day payoff quote from your current lender. Calculate your LTV using this formula: (Loan Balance ÷ Car Value) × 100 = LTV%.

Step 2: Check your credit score. If you are wondering can i refinance my car with a 600 credit score, the answer is yes — but you will need a credit union. At 650, you have more options. Below 600, bring a cosigner.

Step 3: Apply with credit unions first. They are the most flexible with negative equity. Many offer LTV limits up to 130%.

Step 4: Bring a cosigner if needed. This is especially helpful if you are asking can you refinance a car loan with a cosigner — yes, and it works.

⚠️ When refinancing does NOT make sense:
  • Your LTV is above 130% — no lender will approve
  • You have less than 12 months left on your current loan
  • Refinance fees are higher than your total interest savings
  • You are planning to sell the car in the next 6 months
✅ Final Verdict: Should You Refinance When You Owe More Than Your Car Is Worth?

Refinance if: Your LTV is 120% or lower, you can lower your interest rate by at least 2%, you plan to keep the car for another 24+ months, and refinance fees are less than your interest savings.

Do NOT refinance if: Your LTV is above 130%, you have less than 12 months left on your loan, or you are trying to lower payment by extending term (you will pay more total interest).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you refinance a car loan if your upside down? +
Yes, you can refinance a car loan if you're upside down. Most credit unions accept LTV ratios up to 120-130%, meaning you can refinance even if you owe up to 30% more than your car is worth. The calculator above will show you exactly how much you could save.
What are the reasons to refinance your car? +
The main reasons to refinance your car include lowering your monthly payment, reducing your interest rate, shortening your loan term, removing a cosigner from the loan, or switching from a variable to fixed rate. Even if you owe more than the car is worth, refinancing can save you thousands in interest.
When's the best time to refinance a car? +
The best time to refinance a car is when interest rates have dropped at least 1-2% below your current rate, and you have at least 24 months remaining on your loan. Avoid refinancing in the first 6 months of ownership or the last 12 months of your loan term.
Can I refinance my car with a 600 credit score? +
Yes, you can refinance your car with a 600 credit score. Expect higher interest rates between 12-18% APR. Credit unions are your best option, and bringing a cosigner with better credit can help you qualify for a lower rate.
Can I refinance my car with a 650 credit score? +
Yes, at 650 credit score you are in "fair credit" territory. Many lenders will approve you with rates between 8-12% APR. You have more options than at 600, including online lenders, credit unions, and local banks.
Can you refinance a buy here pay here car? +
Yes, you can refinance a buy here pay here car. BHPH dealers charge very high interest rates (15-25% APR). Refinancing with a traditional lender like a credit union can save you thousands. Check your contract for prepayment penalties before refinancing.
Can I refinance a leased car? +
No, you cannot refinance a leased car because you don't own it. However, you can buy out your lease (purchase the car at the residual value) and then refinance that purchase immediately with an auto loan. This two-step process allows you to effectively refinance a leased car.
Can you refinance a car loan with a cosigner? +
Yes, you can refinance a car loan with a cosigner. A cosigner with good credit (700+) can lower your interest rate by 3-5% and improve your approval odds significantly. This is especially helpful if you have negative equity or a credit score below 650.
Refinance Toyota car loan — is it different? +
Refinancing a Toyota car loan works similarly to other cars. Toyota Financial Services offers refinancing for existing Toyota owners, but credit unions and online lenders often offer better rates. Most lenders require 12+ months of on-time payments before approving a Toyota refinance.
